About Me

Hi, my name is Suryaa, and I’m a junior at the UofI. Currently my goal after college is to go into digital product design as a career or through grad school in HCI+Design.
My path into digital product design was incidental. In 9th grade I originally wanted to go into industrial design influenced by Jonathan Ive, but thought I had to be good at sketching, which I was not good at the time. So I moved onto engineering since most of my friends at the time were planning to go into the field. By the time my Senior year of high school came along I applied to mostly computer engineering programs save for a few places with a informatics program. A lot transpired over my senior of high school, I learned about design thinking and interface design. At this point I realized that Computer Engineering might not be the place for me because I started to view myself doing something I was yearning to do (Design) rather than just following others. So with that hope and the options in front of me I came to the University of Illinois. I wasn’t sure what to major in, but all I knew was that I want to break into the design field. I tested my options, but in one standout class till today, Intro to Anthropology, I learned about culture and the components of culture: cognition, behavior, and artifacts. I knew about Cognitive Science through a friend who was majoring in it going into product design, and that was a ah ha moment. More classes lead me to major in Cognitive Psychology with a minor in Informatics. I’m currently interested in Cognitive Science and Complex Systems, which I realized was the perfect connection between my interests in design, technology, and sustainability. Also I’m starting a UI/UX Design Club on campus called Design Innovation Illinois to help my peers self-actualize their careers in design. I hope to understand complex adaptive systems like Earth’s Climate and their challenges then use skills as a design technologist, entrepreneur, and climate activist to hopefully solve them. Get in touch with me about Technology, Ecology, Cognitive Science, Complex Systems, Cybernetics, Design, and anything Arctic-related!
